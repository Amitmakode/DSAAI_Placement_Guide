# 📝 Sample Introduction — Placement Readiness Program

> **Reference examples following the Introduction SOP guidelines**

---

## 🎓 Sample Introduction for Freshers

---

Good morning. Thank you for having me.

My name is **Aryan Mehta**. I'm a fresher Data Scientist, and I'm excited to walk you through my background.

### Education
I did my **B.E. in Computer Science from PICT Pune**, passed out in 2025. Before that, Science stream in 12th from Maharashtra State Board. College is where I first got really interested in data and started taking it seriously.

### Experience
I did a **6-month internship at Hisan Labs Private Limited**. That was my first time working with real business data — not the clean datasets you get online. Real data is very different, and that gap taught me a lot.

### What I Actually Worked On

**Customer Churn Prediction**
- Worked with around **2 lakh records**
- Cleaned the data, created features, tried different models
- Finally went with **Random Forest and XGBoost**
- Achieved **88% accuracy** and **0.91 AUC-ROC**
- That model went live on their internal dashboard

**ETL Pipelines**
- Built ETL pipelines — pulling data from **MySQL**, cleaning it with **Pandas**, and loading it for reporting
- Learned how much work goes into just preparing data before any model

**Power BI Dashboards**
- Made **Power BI dashboards** for the sales team — weekly KPIs, revenue, region-wise numbers
- Earlier they used to do it manually — after my dashboard, it saved them around **40% of that time**

**Final Year Project — Sentiment Analysis**
- Did sentiment analysis using **BERT** — trained on **50,000 product reviews**
- Got **F1-score of 0.86**
- Most of the NLP stuff I learned on my own — reading docs, watching videos, just trying things out

**Kaggle Competitions**
- Titanic and House Price Prediction — mostly for practice
- Helped me get comfortable with the whole process end to end

### Key Skills
- **Python Libraries:** Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, XGBoost for data wrangling, preprocessing, and model building
- **Deep Learning:** TensorFlow, PyTorch, HuggingFace, YOLO
- **Databases:** MySQL, basic MongoDB
- **Tools:** Git, Jupyter, VS Code, Basic cloud for deploying projects
- **Visualization:** Power BI, Excel

### Closing
I know I'm just starting out. But I've not wasted this time — I've built projects, learned things on my own, and made mistakes and fixed them. I'm ready to work hard and learn fast. I'm really looking forward to this opportunity.

That's all from my side. Thank you.

## 💼 Sample Introduction for Experienced (3+ Years)

---

Good morning. Thank you for this opportunity. I'll keep my introduction clear and to the point.

My name is **Aryan Mehta**. I have around **3 years of experience** in the data field — started as a Data Analyst and now working as a Data Scientist. I like working on problems that actually make a difference to the business.

### Education
I did my **B.E. in Computer Science from PICT Pune**, passed out in 2021.

### Current Role
Currently I'm working at **[Current Company]** as a **Data Scientist** in the retail analytics team. Before this I was in e-commerce, so moving into banking was a shift — the data here is more sensitive, the process is more structured, and the stakes are higher. But honestly that's what I wanted — more complex, model-heavy work.

I work closely with the **risk team, product team, and business banking team** on a regular basis.

### What I Work On Here

**Credit Card Default Prediction**
- Main project: identifying customers who might default in the next **90 days** so collections team could reach out early
- Handled this **end to end**: Data extraction from warehouse using SQL, feature engineering on transaction history, repayment behavior, credit utilization
- Modelling with **Logistic Regression, XGBoost, and LightGBM**
- **LightGBM worked best** — approx **87% accuracy, AUC of 0.92**
- Model went into production — in the first quarter itself, **default cases came down by around 12%**

**Customer Segmentation for Cross-Sell**
- Goal: find which savings account customers are most likely to take a personal loan or credit card
- Using **K-Means clustering** and **RFM-based segmentation** on transaction data
- Output goes directly into the marketing team's campaign list
- Instead of mass outreach, they now target the right people — early response rates are looking much better

**Day-to-Day Tools**
- SQL for data extraction
- Python for modelling
- MLflow for experiment tracking
- Git for version control
- AWS S3 and basic PySpark for large data pulls

### Previous Role
Before current company, I was a **Data Analyst** — fast moving, lots of data, decisions needed quickly. Worked with the product, marketing, and supply chain teams regularly.

**Seller Performance Tracking**
- Client has lakhs of sellers — business needed visibility on who was performing, who was dropping off, and why
- Built a **seller scorecard** using SQL and Python — pulling data from multiple sources, cleaning it, and putting it all into a **Power BI dashboard**
- Category heads used that dashboard every week to make decisions on seller support

**Cart Abandonment Analysis**
- A lot of users were adding items to cart but not buying
- Did a full **funnel analysis** — step by step user behavior
- Found a big drop at the payment page in certain pin codes — mainly because COD wasn't available there
- Presented this to the product team and they acted on it
- **Cart conversion improved by around 18%** in those regions within 2 months

**MIS Reports**
- Built weekly and monthly MIS reports for leadership team — revenue, return rates, top categories
- **Automated most of it using Python** so the team didn't have to prepare it manually every week

**Day-to-Day Tools (Previous Role)**
- A lot of SQL, Pandas for data cleaning
- Tableau and Power BI for dashboards
- Excel when stakeholders needed quick views

### Key Skills
- **Python & SQL:** Daily use — Python for cleaning, modelling, and automation; SQL for data extraction and complex queries
- **Data Handling:** Pandas, NumPy
- **Modelling:** Scikit-learn, XGBoost, LightGBM — with hyperparameter tuning using **Optuna** and experiment tracking through **MLFlow**
- **Visualization:** Power BI, Tableau for business dashboards; Plotly for notebook visuals; built a **Streamlit app** for direct business use
- **Infrastructure:** MySQL, basic PySpark for large data; Git and GitHub for version control
- **Core Skills:** Machine learning, data preprocessing, data presentation, data pipelines, and understanding data in easy ways to solve problems

### Closing
So that's my journey — from analyzing data in e-commerce to building and deploying models in banking. In both places the work was connected to real business outcomes, not just model accuracy. I enjoy working with different teams, understanding what they actually need, and then solving it with data.

I'm hoping to bring that same approach here. Thank you.

## ⚡ Key Points to Remember

| Guideline | Fresher | Experienced |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Duration** | Minimum 5 minutes | Minimum 5 minutes |
| **Focus** | Projects + Internship R&R | Current & Previous Role R&R |
| **Tense** | Past / Present Continuous | Past / Present Continuous |
| **Avoid** | "I can...", "I will...", "I want to..." | Client names, Project names |
| **Include** | Metrics, Tools Used, Business Impact | Metrics, Tools Used, Business Impact |
